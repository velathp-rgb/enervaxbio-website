How it's made

From waste oil to on-spec fuel, in five controlled steps.

Used cooking oil is a variable feedstock. Our process is built to handle that variability methodically rather than guess at it batch to batch.

01

Collect

Used cooking oil is collected from residences, restaurants, hotels, and industrial kitchens in sealed containers, with volumes logged at intake.

02

Pre-treat

High-FFA feedstock is routed through acid esterification first, so acidity doesn't compromise the main conversion stage.

03

Convert

Transesterification under controlled temperature and catalyst dosing splits the oil into crude biodiesel and glycerol.

04

Refine

Washing, vacuum drying, and methanol recovery strip the crude ester down to finished, on-spec fuel.

05

Blend to order

Finished biodiesel is cut to the ratio your order specifies — anywhere from B5 to B100 — and released against contracted offtake.

Common questions

Biodiesel, plainly explained.

The short, honest answers to what people usually ask before placing a first order.

What actually is biodiesel?

A renewable fuel made by converting fats and oils — in our case, used cooking oil — into fatty acid methyl esters through a chemical process called transesterification. It behaves like diesel in an engine, because chemically it's a close cousin of it.

Will it damage my engine, or do I need to modify anything?

At low blends (B5–B20) most diesel engines run on it with zero modification. At higher blends, including B100, compatibility depends on your specific engine and OEM guidance — we'll talk through that with you before sizing an order, rather than assume one answer fits every fleet.

How does Enervax Bio actually make it?

We collect used cooking oil, pre-treat the high-acidity portion through acid esterification, then run it through transesterification, washing, drying, and methanol recovery on our own skid-built plant in Ajman. We're not a trading desk relabeling someone else's fuel.

What does "order-based blending" mean?

Instead of selling only one fixed product, we cut biodiesel into diesel at the ratio your purchase order specifies — anywhere between B5 and B100 — so the blend matches your fleet's needs and your sustainability targets, not a one-size-fits-all SKU.

Is it actually better for the environment, or is that just marketing?

Used-cooking-oil-based biodiesel is widely recognised as a genuine circular-economy fuel, with lifecycle greenhouse-gas savings that scale with the blend ratio. We've shown indicative figures above; we're holding off on precise, certified figures for our own output until our own testing and ESMA certification are complete, rather than borrow someone else's numbers.

Is biodiesel use approved in the UAE?

Yes — biodiesel blending into diesel is permitted under UAE fuel regulations, and the country's 2024 National Biofuels Policy actively encourages local production. We're pursuing ESMA certification for our own output as we move into contracted supply.
